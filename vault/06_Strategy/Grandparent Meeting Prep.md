# Grandparent Company Meeting Preparation

---

## Objective

Present findings to ATI's parent company to secure:
1. **Investment capital** for Apollo (top ask)
2. **Independent financial controls** — Apollo needs direct access to its own bank accounts and accounting system
3. **Audit trail implementation** on the accounting system
4. **Independent review** of ATI's management of Apollo's finances

---

## Opening Statement

> Walker should say this first — sets the frame for the entire meeting:

**"I'm bringing this to you because I believe you'd want to know. Over the past two years, I've been told Apollo is losing money and needs to borrow from ATI. But when I got access to the bank statements and accounting records, the numbers tell a different story. Apollo has had over $900,000 extracted from its bank account in 'paydowns' on debts I never agreed to, and its fuel costs are being inflated by 83% above what we actually pay. I'm not here to make accusations — I'm here to show you the data and ask for your help fixing it."**

This framing:
- Positions Walker as acting in the grandparent's interest (not adversarial)
- Leads with the dollar amount (gets attention)
- Mentions independent verification upfront (establishes credibility)
- Asks for help, not punishment (reduces defensiveness)

## Anticipated Pushback + Counter-Arguments

### "These are normal intercompany accounting practices"
**Counter:** Normal intercompany accounting doesn't inflate fuel costs by 83% above independently verified amounts. Normal accounting doesn't backdate entries by 79-476 days. Normal accounting has an audit trail. The directional consistency — every anomaly inflates Apollo's costs or ATI's receivables — rules out innocent explanation.

### "It's just sloppy bookkeeping, not intentional"
**Counter:** If errors were random, they'd go in both directions. Every single anomaly we've found moves money the same way: Apollo loses, ATI gains. The probability of 10+ independent "errors" all favoring the same party by chance is negligible. Additionally, the audit trail was never turned on — a deliberate choice that enables manipulation.

### "Walker doesn't understand accounting"
**Counter:** The analysis is based on bank statements (which Walker didn't prepare), EFS fuel card records (third-party), and IFTA government filings (sworn). These are independently verified facts, not Walker's interpretation. The accounting system data corroborates but is not the primary evidence.

### "The promissory note was a legitimate business arrangement that was just recorded late"
**Counter:** A 174-day delay in recording an $858K obligation is not administrative lag. The note appeared on the balance sheet at the same time as the Wire Payable ($870K) disappeared — suggesting it was a relabeling of existing amounts, not new debt. No executed copy of the note has been produced.

### "Apollo legitimately owes this money for fuel, insurance, and services"
**Counter:** Show them Exhibit 2. Apollo's actual fuel payments (bank-verified) are $506K. The P&L says $929K. The $422K difference exists entirely in manual journal entries with no supporting invoices. If the charges were legitimate, there would be invoices.

### "Gemini is a separate business; its finances are not relevant to Apollo"
**Counter:** Gemini's "Note Receivable from Apollo" grew by $213K in 24 months — more than Gemini's total net income ($128K). If Gemini and Apollo were truly separate, Apollo's debt to Gemini should not grow faster than Gemini's income. Customer payments to Apollo are being reclassified as "Gemini overpayments."

## Questions to Ask THEM (Force Admissions)

These questions are designed so that any answer either confirms the finding or creates a documented contradiction:

1. **"Can you provide the executed copy of the $858,000 promissory note, with both signatures and a notary?"**
   - If produced: compare date to QB timestamp (174-day gap)
   - If not produced: the note may not exist as a legitimate document

2. **"What is the basis for the $1.2 million in paydowns Apollo made to ATI? Can you show us the loan agreements?"**
   - If produced: compare terms to actual transfers
   - If not produced: $1.2M was extracted without documentation

3. **"Why does Apollo's P&L show $929K in fuel when EFS records show $506K was actually paid?"**
   - Any answer must address the $422K gap. "Journal entries" is not an answer without invoices.

4. **"Why was the accounting system audit trail never enabled?"**
   - There is no good answer to this. An audit trail is a basic internal control.

5. **"Who authorized the deletion of 23 Gemini records by Cordee?"**
   - If Cordee acted alone: why does an Apollo team member have unsupervised access to delete records?
   - If directed: who directed it and why?

6. **"Can you explain why all 12 monthly financial statements were printed on the same date?"**
   - Batch printing means the statements were generated retrospectively, not contemporaneously.

7. **"Rosanna controls 47.5% of all journal entries and is the sole bank authorizer. Is this appropriate segregation of duties?"**
   - The answer is no, by any accounting standard.

3. **Within 30 days:** Enable audit trail on the QuickBooks system
4. **Within 60 days:** Restructure Apollo's financial controls so that no single person (Rosanna) controls both journal entries and bank authorizations
